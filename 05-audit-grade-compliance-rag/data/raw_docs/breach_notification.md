# Breach Notification Policy

**Policy ID:** BREACH-001
**Version:** 1.0 (CURRENT)
**Owner:** Privacy Office / Legal
**Applies to:** All Aventine Health Network workforce members

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines how Aventine Health Network responds to and reports a breach of unsecured protected health information (PHI), in compliance with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.

## 2. Definition of a Breach
A breach is the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of unsecured PHI in a manner not permitted by the HIPAA Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of the PHI, unless a risk assessment demonstrates a low probability that the PHI has been compromised.

## 3. Internal Reporting
Any workforce member who suspects a breach must report it to the Privacy Office within one (1) hour of discovery through the Incident Portal. Do not attempt to investigate independently.

## 4. Notification to Individuals
Affected individuals must be notified without unreasonable delay and no later than **sixty (60) days** after discovery of the breach.

## 5. Notification to Regulators
Breaches affecting 500 or more individuals must be reported to the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and prominent media outlets without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days after discovery. Smaller breaches are logged and reported to HHS annually.

## 6. Documentation
The Privacy Office maintains a breach log documenting the risk assessment, affected individuals, and notifications sent for every reported incident.
