# Patient Records Data Retention Policy

**Policy ID:** RETEN-002
**Version:** 2.0 (CURRENT)
**Owner:** Health Information Management / Compliance
**Applies to:** All Aventine Health Network staff handling patient records

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes how long Aventine Health Network (AHN) retains patient medical records and associated protected health information (PHI) before secure destruction, in order to meet clinical, legal, and regulatory obligations. This version 2.0 supersedes RETEN-001 and extends the standard retention period.

## 2. Standard Retention Period
Adult patient medical records must be retained for a minimum of **ten (10) years** from the date of the patient's last treatment or discharge. This change from the previous six-year period reflects updated state regulatory guidance and AHN's expanded clinical research obligations. After this period, records that are not subject to a legal hold may be securely destroyed.

## 3. Minor Patient Records
Records of patients who were minors at the time of treatment must be retained until the patient reaches age 23, or ten (10) years after last treatment, whichever is longer.

## 4. Billing and Claims Records
Billing records, insurance claims, and remittance data must be retained for ten (10) years from the date the claim was resolved.

## 5. Destruction Procedure
Records eligible for destruction must be destroyed using an approved secure-shredding vendor, and a certificate of destruction must be logged in the Records Management System. A second reviewer must countersign every destruction batch under this version of the policy.

## 6. Legal Holds
Any record subject to litigation, audit, or investigation is exempt from routine destruction until the hold is formally released by the Legal department.
