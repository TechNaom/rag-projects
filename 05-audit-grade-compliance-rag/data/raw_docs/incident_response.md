# Security Incident Response Policy

**Policy ID:** INC-001
**Version:** 1.0 (CURRENT)
**Owner:** Information Security Incident Response Team (CIRT)
**Applies to:** All Aventine Health Network workforce members

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes how Aventine Health Network detects, responds to, and recovers from information security incidents affecting its systems or data.

## 2. Incident Classification
Incidents are classified by severity: Sev-1 (confirmed compromise of ePHI or critical systems), Sev-2 (suspected compromise or significant service disruption), and Sev-3 (minor or contained events). Classification determines escalation timelines.

## 3. Reporting and Escalation
Any suspected incident must be reported to the Incident Response Team within one (1) hour of discovery. Sev-1 incidents trigger immediate notification of the Privacy Office, Legal, and executive leadership.

## 4. Containment and Eradication
The Incident Response Team leads containment to limit the scope of an incident, followed by eradication of the root cause. Affected credentials must be reset and compromised accounts disabled.

## 5. Coordination with Breach Notification
If an incident involves unsecured PHI, the Incident Response Team coordinates with the Privacy Office to determine whether the Breach Notification Policy applies and to meet regulatory reporting deadlines.

## 6. Post-Incident Review
A documented post-incident review must be completed within ten (10) business days of resolution, capturing root cause, timeline, and corrective actions to prevent recurrence.
