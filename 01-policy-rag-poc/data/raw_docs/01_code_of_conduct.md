# Northkeep National Bank — Employee Code of Conduct

**Policy ID:** HR-COC-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Human Resources & Corporate Compliance
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, and temporary staff

## 1. Purpose
This Code of Conduct sets the minimum standard of professional and ethical behavior expected of everyone who works at Northkeep National Bank. As a federally regulated financial institution, Northkeep holds itself to a higher standard of integrity than most employers, because the trust our customers place in us is the foundation of our business.

## 2. Core Principles
Employees are expected to act with honesty, fairness, and respect in every interaction — with customers, colleagues, regulators, and the public. Employees must never use their position at the Bank for personal gain at the expense of a customer or the institution.

## 3. Conduct With Customers
Employees must provide accurate, complete information about products and fees. Employees may never recommend a product primarily because it generates higher commission if a lower-cost product better serves the customer's stated needs. Misrepresenting account terms, interest rates, or fee structures is grounds for immediate termination.

## 4. Workplace Behavior
Employees are expected to treat colleagues with respect regardless of role, seniority, or department. Harassment, discrimination, bullying, or retaliation of any kind will not be tolerated (see Policy HR-EEO-004 for full detail).

## 5. Use of Bank Resources
Bank systems, email, and physical resources are provided for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted within reason, but employees may not use Bank resources for outside business activities, political campaigning, or any activity that could embarrass the institution.

## 6. Gifts and Entertainment
Employees may accept gifts from customers or vendors only if the value is below $100 USD per year per source, and the gift is not cash or a cash equivalent. Gifts above this threshold must be declined or reported to the employee's manager and logged with Compliance. Employees in lending or procurement roles are subject to a stricter $50 annual threshold due to conflict-of-interest risk.

## 7. Reporting Violations
Any employee who becomes aware of a violation of this Code — by themselves or others — must report it to their manager, HR Business Partner, or the Ethics Hotline (1-800-555-0199, available 24/7, anonymous reporting available). Retaliation against a good-faith reporter is itself a violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action up to termination.

## 8. Consequences of Violation
Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action ranging from a documented warning to immediate termination, depending on severity, and may also be reported to regulators where required by law (e.g., FINRA, OCC).

## 9. Acknowledgment
All employees must read and electronically acknowledge this Code annually as part of mandatory compliance training (see Policy COMP-TRN-009).
