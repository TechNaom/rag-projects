# Northkeep National Bank — Performance Review & Promotion Policy

**Policy ID:** HR-PERF-012
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Human Resources — Talent Management
**Applies to:** All employees

## 1. Performance Review Cycle
Northkeep operates an annual formal review cycle (October–December for the prior 12 months), with a mid-year informal check-in (May–June). Reviews are conducted in the PerformHR system and require both a self-assessment and a manager assessment.

## 2. Rating Scale
Employees are rated on a 5-point scale:
1. Does Not Meet Expectations
2. Partially Meets Expectations
3. Meets Expectations
4. Exceeds Expectations
5. Significantly Exceeds Expectations

Ratings of 1 or 2 automatically trigger a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) discussion with HR within 10 business days of the rating being finalized.

## 3. Compliance Conduct Gate
Regardless of business performance rating, any employee with a substantiated Code of Conduct, compliance, or risk violation in the review period is capped at a maximum overall rating of \"3 — Meets Expectations,\" and is ineligible for promotion or a rating above 3 in that cycle, reflecting Northkeep's principle that strong results do not offset poor conduct.

## 4. Merit Increases and Bonus
Merit increases and annual bonus pool eligibility are tied to the final rating and are typically communicated and effective in March following the review cycle. Employees who joined after October 1 of the review year are not eligible for that cycle's bonus but are eligible for the following cycle.

## 5. Promotion Process
Promotions require: (a) a rating of \"Exceeds Expectations\" or higher in the most recent cycle, (b) at least 12 months in the current role (waivable by VP+ for exceptional cases), and (c) calibration review by the manager's leadership team to ensure consistency across the department. Promotions to Director level and above additionally require review by the Talent Committee.

## 6. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
A PIP is a structured 60-90 day plan with specific, measurable goals, set jointly by the manager and HR. Employees on a PIP receive bi-weekly check-ins. At the end of the PIP period, the employee either returns to standard performance management, is extended for one additional 30-day period in exceptional circumstances, or is separated from the Bank if goals are not met.

## 7. Calibration
To ensure rating consistency across teams, each department holds a calibration session before ratings are finalized, where managers review and adjust ratings relative to peers at the same level performing similar work.

## 8. Appeals
Employees who believe their rating was applied inconsistently with this policy (e.g., not properly calibrated, conduct gate misapplied) may request a documented review by their HR Business Partner within 15 business days of receiving their final rating.
