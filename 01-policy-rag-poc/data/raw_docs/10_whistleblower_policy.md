# Northkeep National Bank — Whistleblower Policy

**Policy ID:** COMP-WB-010
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Corporate Compliance / Legal
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, and former employees

## 1. Purpose
This policy encourages employees to report suspected violations of law, regulation, or internal policy without fear of retaliation, and explains the protections available to them.

## 2. What to Report
Employees should report concerns regarding: violations of banking laws or regulations (BSA/AML, fair lending, securities law), accounting or financial reporting irregularities, fraud against the Bank or its customers, unsafe or unsound banking practices, and any of the conduct issues described in the Code of Conduct (Policy HR-COC-001).

## 3. Reporting Channels
Concerns may be reported through any of the following:
- Direct manager or skip-level manager
- Corporate Compliance or Internal Audit
- The Ethics & Conduct Hotline (1-800-555-0199), available 24/7, with an option for fully anonymous reporting via the EthicsPoint third-party portal
- Directly to a federal regulator (OCC, CFPB, SEC, or FinCEN), which employees are legally entitled to do at any time without first reporting internally

## 4. Anti-Retaliation Protection
Northkeep strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports a concern in good faith, including termination, demotion, pay reduction, exclusion from meetings or assignments, or harassment. \"Good faith\" means the employee reasonably believed the information to be true at the time of reporting — the report does not need to be ultimately substantiated to be protected. Employees who believe they have experienced retaliation should report it immediately through the same channels above; retaliation complaints are treated as high-priority and investigated by Legal, not the reporting employee's chain of command.

## 5. Confidentiality
Northkeep will make reasonable efforts to keep the identity of the reporting employee confidential, limited to those who need to know to conduct the investigation, except where disclosure is required by law or necessary for a fair investigation (e.g., the respondent's due process rights).

## 6. Investigation
Reports are triaged within 5 business days. Reports involving senior executives, financial reporting, or potential criminal conduct are escalated to the Audit Committee of the Board and may involve outside counsel.

## 7. Legal Protections
Employees are protected under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act, Dodd-Frank Act (including potential whistleblower awards for original information reported directly to the SEC leading to a successful enforcement action), and applicable state whistleblower laws. Nothing in this policy, any employment agreement, or any severance agreement restricts an employee's right to report concerns to a government agency or to receive a whistleblower award.

## 8. False Reports
This policy protects good-faith reports, not reports known by the reporter to be false or made with reckless disregard for the truth. Knowingly false reports made in bad faith may result in disciplinary action.
