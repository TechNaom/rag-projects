# Northkeep National Bank — Equal Opportunity & Anti-Harassment Policy

**Policy ID:** HR-EEO-004
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Human Resources / Legal
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, applicants, and interns

## 1. Equal Employment Opportunity
Northkeep National Bank is committed to providing equal employment opportunity to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, genetic information, or any other status protected by applicable federal, state, or local law. This applies to hiring, promotion, compensation, termination, and all other terms of employment.

## 2. Prohibited Harassment
Harassment includes any unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment, or that is made a condition of employment (quid pro quo). This includes verbal conduct (slurs, jokes, comments), physical conduct (unwanted touching), and visual conduct (offensive images, gestures). Sexual harassment specifically includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies in the workplace, at Bank-sponsored events, on business travel, and in any work-related communication, including email, chat, and social media, regardless of whether it occurs during or outside normal business hours.

## 4. Reporting Process
Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination should report it through any of the following channels:
- Their direct manager (unless the manager is the subject of the complaint)
- HR Business Partner
- The confidential Ethics & Conduct Hotline (1-800-555-0199), available 24/7, including anonymous reporting
- Legal/Employee Relations directly

Reports may be made verbally or in writing. Employees are not required to report to their direct manager if the manager is involved.

## 5. Investigation Process
All reports are investigated promptly, typically within 10 business days for initial assessment, by HR Employee Relations or an external investigator for senior leadership complaints. Investigations are conducted with as much confidentiality as possible while allowing for a thorough and fair process. Both the complainant and respondent are informed of the outcome to the extent appropriate.

## 6. Non-Retaliation
Retaliation against any employee for reporting harassment or discrimination in good faith, or for participating in an investigation, is strictly prohibited and is itself a violation subject to discipline up to termination, regardless of the outcome of the underlying complaint.

## 7. Disciplinary Action
Substantiated violations result in disciplinary action proportionate to severity, ranging from coaching and mandatory training to immediate termination for serious or repeated violations.

## 8. Manager Responsibilities
Managers who become aware of potential harassment or discrimination — whether reported directly to them or observed — must escalate to HR within 2 business days. Failure to escalate is itself a policy violation.
