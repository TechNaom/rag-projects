# Northkeep National Bank — Data Privacy & Client Confidentiality Policy

**Policy ID:** COMP-DP-008
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Corporate Compliance / Information Security
**Applies to:** All employees and contractors with access to customer data

## 1. Purpose
This policy protects customer Non-Public Personal Information (NPI) in compliance with the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), state privacy laws, and Northkeep's contractual obligations to customers.

## 2. What Counts as NPI
NPI includes: account numbers, Social Security numbers, balances, transaction history, income information, and any other personally identifiable financial information a customer provides that is not otherwise publicly available.

## 3. Access on a Need-to-Know Basis
Employees may only access customer NPI when necessary to perform their job duties. Browsing customer accounts out of curiosity — including accounts of friends, family, celebrities, or coworkers — is strictly prohibited and is treated as a serious violation even if no data is shared externally (\"unauthorized access\" alone is a violation, regardless of intent). All access to core banking systems is logged and subject to audit.

## 4. Sharing Customer Information
Customer NPI may not be shared with anyone outside the Bank, including the customer's own family members, without the customer's documented consent or a valid legal basis (subpoena, court order, regulatory request processed through Legal). Internally, NPI should only be shared with colleagues who have a legitimate business need.

## 5. Data Handling Standards
- Customer data must never be stored on personal devices, personal email, or personal cloud storage (Dropbox, personal Google Drive, etc.)
- Spreadsheets or reports containing NPI must be encrypted and access-restricted before being emailed internally
- Printed documents containing NPI must be retrieved immediately from shared printers and shredded when no longer needed
- USB drives and other removable media may not be used to transfer customer data without Information Security approval

## 6. Third-Party Vendors
Any vendor or service provider with access to customer NPI must be vetted through the Third-Party Risk Management process and bound by a signed data protection agreement before access is granted.

## 7. Data Breach Reporting
Any suspected or actual unauthorized access, loss, or exposure of customer NPI must be reported to the Information Security Incident Response team immediately (within 1 hour of discovery) via the internal Incident Portal or the Security Hotline. Do not attempt to independently investigate or remediate a suspected breach before reporting — this can compromise evidence and regulatory response timelines.

## 8. Consequences
Unauthorized access to or disclosure of customer NPI is grounds for immediate termination and may result in civil or criminal liability for the employee under GLBA and related state laws, independent of any action taken by the Bank.

## 9. Training
All employees with system access to customer data complete Data Privacy & Confidentiality training within 14 days of hire and annually thereafter as part of mandatory compliance training (Policy COMP-TRN-009).
