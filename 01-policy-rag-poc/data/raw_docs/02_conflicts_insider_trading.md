# Northkeep National Bank — Conflicts of Interest & Insider Trading Policy

**Policy ID:** COMP-COI-002
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Corporate Compliance / Legal
**Applies to:** All employees; enhanced obligations for Investment Banking, Wealth Management, Treasury, and Executive staff

## 1. Purpose
This policy protects Northkeep National Bank, its customers, and the broader market from conflicts of interest and the misuse of material non-public information (MNPI).

## 2. Identifying a Conflict of Interest
A conflict of interest exists whenever an employee's personal interests — financial, familial, or otherwise — could reasonably be seen to influence their professional judgment. Common examples include: approving a loan for a family member, holding a significant financial stake in a vendor the employee selects, or serving on the board of a company that competes with or supplies the Bank.

## 3. Disclosure Requirement
Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and to Compliance via the Conflicts Disclosure Portal within 5 business days of becoming aware of it. Annual conflict-of-interest attestations are mandatory for all employees at Director level and above.

## 4. Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)
Employees who, in the course of their work, come into possession of MNPI about Northkeep, a customer, or a counterparty — including pending mergers, undisclosed earnings, regulatory actions, or large pending transactions — are strictly prohibited from:
- Trading securities of the affected company while in possession of MNPI
- Sharing (\"tipping\") that information with anyone, including friends or family, who might trade on it
- Using the information to advise on personal investment decisions

## 5. Restricted and Watch Lists
Compliance maintains a Restricted List of securities employees may not trade and a Watch List subject to enhanced monitoring. Employees in Investment Banking, Research, and Treasury must check the Restricted List before placing any personal trade and pre-clear trades through the Personal Trading Compliance system.

## 6. Outside Business Activities
Employees must obtain written approval from Compliance before accepting outside employment, board seats, or significant business ownership stakes, particularly where the outside entity does business with Northkeep or operates in financial services.

## 7. Gifts From Customers Seeking Credit Decisions
Employees involved in credit or lending decisions are prohibited from accepting any gift, favor, or benefit from an applicant or borrower during the underwriting period, regardless of value.

## 8. Violations
Insider trading is a federal crime under the Securities Exchange Act and may result in termination, regulatory referral to the SEC/FINRA, and personal criminal liability. Conflicts-of-interest violations that do not rise to insider trading are addressed through HR disciplinary process and may still result in termination.

## 9. Training
All employees complete mandatory Conflicts of Interest & Insider Trading training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter.
