What gets deeper here

Beyond definitions

You move from “what is RAG?” into when chunking fails, how retrieval quality degrades, why re-ranking changes outcomes, and what production constraints force different choices.

What you can defend

By the end, you should be able to explain why a specific retrieval stack was chosen, what it optimizes for, which risks remain, and how you would prove it works.

Executive framing

This course is organized around decisions, not just topics.

Each section pulls the learner toward the real questions teams face when deploying RAG: what to index, how to chunk, which retrieval path to trust, how to measure quality, and when extra complexity is actually justified.

Failure-mode first

Start from hallucinations, stale context, weak recall, and noisy retrieval so every concept is anchored to a concrete system problem.

Trade-off driven

Coverage, latency, cost, operational complexity, and user trust are treated as design constraints rather than afterthoughts.

Evidence over slogans

Quizzes, labs, notebooks, and projects are there to make you show what a design does, not just repeat terminology.

What the learner journey covers

From foundations to production posture.

The flow moves in layers: first why retrieval is needed, then how data becomes retrievable, then how search quality is improved, and finally how the system behaves under operational pressure.

FoundationsModules 1–3 establish why retrieval exists and how documents are shaped for it.
Core retrievalModules 4–8 cover embeddings, vector systems, retrieval stacks, re-ranking, and prompt grounding.
Operational depthModules 9–12 handle evaluation, production concerns, advanced patterns, and portfolio-grade builds.
