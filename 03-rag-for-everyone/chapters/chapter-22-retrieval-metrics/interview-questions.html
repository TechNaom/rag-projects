Interview Questions: Retrieval Metrics

Q01

Why evaluate retrieval separately?

To know whether bad answers came from missing evidence or generation behavior.

Q02

What is Recall@K?

The fraction of gold evidence retrieved within the top K results.

Q03

What is Precision@K?

The fraction of top K retrieved results that are relevant.

Q04

What is MRR?

The average reciprocal rank of the first relevant result across queries.

Q05

When is nDCG useful?

When relevance is graded and rank position matters.

Q06

Why slice metrics?

Averages can hide failures by domain, query type, source format, tenant, or risk.

Q07

What does high recall but low MRR suggest?

Gold evidence is present but ranked too low, so reranking or ranking tuning may help.

Q08

What is filtered-out gold?

Relevant evidence removed by metadata, access, freshness, or tenant filters before final ranking.

Q09

What should a retrieval dashboard include?

Quality metrics, latency, empty retrievals, filter drops, stale hits, and version metadata.

Q10

What is the senior answer?

Use retrieval metrics as diagnostic tools tied to concrete tuning actions and release gates.
