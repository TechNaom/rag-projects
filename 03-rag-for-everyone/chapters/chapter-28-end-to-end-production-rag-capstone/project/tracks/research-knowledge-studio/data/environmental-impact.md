# Environmental Impact Estimate

This estimate models greenhouse gas effects over a ten-year horizon for each option, using Riverton's current grid mix and travel patterns.

## Emissions Per Trip

An e-bike trip that replaces a car trip saves about 0.15 kg of CO2. Because e-bike batteries charge on a grid that is still 40 percent fossil fuel, the bikes are not zero-emission, but they are close.

A BRT trip that replaces a car trip saves about 0.55 kg of CO2 per rider, and a full articulated bus removes far more cars from the road per vehicle than any single bike.

## Total Annual Reduction

The key variable is how many car trips each option actually replaces. The e-bike program is projected to displace about 900 car trips per day. BRT is projected to displace about 5,600 car trips per day because it carries far more people and pulls from longer, car-dominated commutes.

## Net Effect

On projected volumes, BRT delivers roughly six times the annual CO2 reduction of the e-bike program, despite higher per-vehicle emissions. This partly disagrees with the cost analysis: the cheapest option to launch is not the largest climate lever.

## Caveat

If e-bike ridership substantially exceeds projections, or if the grid decarbonizes faster than planned, the gap narrows. These numbers are sensitive to adoption assumptions and should be revisited yearly.
