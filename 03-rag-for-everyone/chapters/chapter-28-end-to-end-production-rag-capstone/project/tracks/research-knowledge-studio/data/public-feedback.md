# Public Feedback Summary

Feedback was gathered from four public meetings and 830 written comments. This summary captures the dominant themes, not a statistical sample.

## Support For E-Bikes

Younger residents and downtown workers strongly favored e-bikes, citing speed, fun, and freedom from parking. Local businesses near the proposed hubs were broadly supportive.

## Concern About E-Bikes

Disability advocates raised the loudest objection: dockless bikes left on sidewalks block wheelchair paths and accessible ramps. Several older residents said they simply would not use bikes and felt the program spent public money on something not built for them.

## Support For BRT

Residents of outer neighborhoods and commuters favored BRT, describing it as something the whole family could rely on in any weather. Disability advocates preferred BRT for its level boarding and predictable service.

## The Different Angle

A recurring written comment did not fit the either-or framing at all: residents asked why the city was treating this as a competition. Many wanted a small e-bike pilot as a downtown connector feeding into an upgraded bus network. This "both, sequenced" view was the single most common written suggestion and matches what Brentwell eventually did.
