# Maintenance and Operations Report

This report projects the day-to-day operational load of each option, based on vendor data and peer-city figures.

## E-Bike Operations

A dockless fleet needs constant field labor. Crews must recharge or swap batteries, rebalance bikes from where they pile up to where they are needed, and repair damage. Peer cities report that 10 to 15 percent of a fleet is out of service on any given day. Bikes have a useful life of about three years before replacement.

## BRT Operations

BRT concentrates maintenance in a central depot with trained mechanics and a predictable schedule. Buses have a useful life of twelve to fifteen years. The operational risk is different: a single driver shortage or depot problem can disrupt an entire corridor, whereas a broken bike removes only one vehicle from service.

## Reliability For Riders

BRT offers a published schedule riders can plan around. Dockless e-bikes offer no guarantee that a working bike will be nearby when a rider needs one, which peer surveys flag as the top complaint.

## Operational Verdict

E-bikes are cheaper to run but operationally noisier, with continuous distributed labor. BRT is costlier to run but more predictable and longer-lived. This reinforces the case study: the two systems fail in different ways and are not simple substitutes.
