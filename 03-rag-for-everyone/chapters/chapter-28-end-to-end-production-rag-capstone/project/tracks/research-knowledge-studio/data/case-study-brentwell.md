# Case Study: Brentwell's Dockless E-Bike Rollout

Brentwell is a comparable mid-size city of about 200,000 residents that launched a dockless e-bike program four years ago. Its experience is the closest real-world analog available to Riverton.

## What Went Well

Ridership beat projections in year one, reaching 4,300 trips per day. Downtown retail reported modest gains in foot traffic. The program launched in nine months, far faster than any road-building project.

## What Went Wrong

Bike clutter on sidewalks generated hundreds of complaints and one lawsuit over blocked accessible curb ramps. Brentwell had to add designated parking corrals in year two at unplanned cost. Vandalism and theft removed about 12 percent of the fleet from service in the first eighteen months.

## Equity Finding

Usage clustered heavily in the wealthier, flatter downtown. Outer and hillier neighborhoods saw almost no ridership, which drew criticism that the program mainly served people who already had good transit options.

## Lesson For Riverton

Brentwell's takeaway was not "e-bikes failed." It was that e-bikes succeeded as a downtown convenience layer but did not function as a backbone for citywide mobility. Brentwell is now pairing the bikes with a bus upgrade rather than choosing one over the other.
