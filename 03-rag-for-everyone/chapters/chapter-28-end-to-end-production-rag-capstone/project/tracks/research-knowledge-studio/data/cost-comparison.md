# Cost Comparison: Dockless E-Bikes vs. Bus Rapid Transit

Prepared for the Riverton Transportation Committee. Riverton has roughly 180,000 residents and a current annual transit budget of $42 million.

## Capital Cost

A dockless e-bike program covering the downtown core and three inner neighborhoods needs about 1,200 bikes at $1,300 each, plus charging and parking hubs. Total up-front capital is about $2.4 million.

Expanding bus rapid transit (BRT) along the two busiest corridors requires dedicated bus lanes, signal priority, level boarding platforms, and eight new articulated buses. Total up-front capital is about $38 million, most of it in road and platform construction.

## Operating Cost

E-bike operating cost runs about $1.9 million per year, driven mostly by field crews who rebalance, recharge, and repair bikes.

BRT operating cost runs about $6.5 million per year, mostly driver wages, fuel, and vehicle maintenance.

## Cost Per Trip

At projected ridership, the e-bike program lands near $1.85 per trip. BRT lands near $2.40 per trip. Both are below the $3.10 per-trip subsidy the city already pays on its lowest-performing local routes.

## Bottom Line

On pure cost-to-launch, e-bikes win by a wide margin: roughly one-sixteenth the capital outlay. But this analysis only counts direct program cost. It does not weigh how many people each option actually moves, nor who is left out, which the ridership and feedback studies address.
