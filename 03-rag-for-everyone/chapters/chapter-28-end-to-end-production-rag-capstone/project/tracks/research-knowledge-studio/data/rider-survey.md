# Rider Survey Summary

A stratified survey of 2,100 Riverton residents ran over six weeks. Margin of error is about 2 percent.

## Willingness to Use

62 percent of respondents said they would use a dockless e-bike at least occasionally, but that number drops to 34 percent among residents over 60 and to 21 percent among residents who report a mobility disability.

71 percent said they would use improved bus rapid transit, and that figure stays above 60 percent across every age group and disability status.

## Trip Purpose

E-bike interest concentrates on short trips under three miles: errands, commutes downtown, and connections to existing transit. BRT interest concentrates on longer commutes and trips carrying groceries, children, or work equipment.

## Weather Sensitivity

E-bike willingness falls sharply in the survey's winter scenario. Only 28 percent said they would ride a bike in the January cold, while stated BRT use barely moved.

## Key Divergence

The survey's main finding is that the two options serve different riders. E-bikes appeal to younger, shorter-trip, fair-weather users. BRT appeals more evenly across age, ability, and weather. Treating the two as interchangeable would misread the demand.
