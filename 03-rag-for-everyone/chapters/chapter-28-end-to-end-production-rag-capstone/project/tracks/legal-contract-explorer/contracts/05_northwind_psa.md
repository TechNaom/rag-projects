# Contract: Northwind Payroll Services Corp. — Professional Services Agreement

## 1. Term and Termination
This Agreement remains in effect for twelve (12) months and renews for successive one-year terms unless either party provides forty-five (45) days written notice of non-renewal. Either party may terminate for convenience with ninety (90) days prior written notice. Because payroll is a business-critical function, Northwind shall provide up to sixty (60) days of transition assistance following any termination to migrate the Client to a successor provider.

## 2. Limitation of Liability
Northwind Payroll Services' aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed two times (2x) the total fees paid by the Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. This cap does not apply to Northwind's liability for tax penalties or interest assessed against the Client that result directly from Northwind's calculation errors, which shall be Northwind's sole responsibility.

## 3. Indemnification
Northwind Payroll Services shall indemnify and hold harmless the Client from third-party claims and regulatory penalties arising from Northwind's failure to remit payroll taxes accurately and on time, except to the extent such failure is caused by inaccurate or late information supplied by the Client. The Client shall indemnify Northwind against claims arising from data the Client knew to be false.

## 4. Payment Terms
Fees are invoiced per payroll cycle and are due within net twenty (20) days of the invoice date. Northwind Payroll Services debits the Client's designated account for statutory tax remittances one (1) business day before each filing deadline. A returned or failed debit incurs a fifty dollar ($50) processing fee plus any regulatory penalties.

## 5. Confidentiality
Northwind shall protect all employee personal data and Client payroll records as strictly Confidential Information and shall comply with all applicable data protection laws. Confidentiality obligations regarding employee personal data survive indefinitely. Northwind shall not use Client payroll data for any purpose other than delivering the Services.

## 6. Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, without regard to conflict-of-laws rules. Any dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered in Boston, Massachusetts under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.
