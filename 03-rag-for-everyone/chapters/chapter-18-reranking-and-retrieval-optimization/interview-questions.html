Interview Questions: Reranking and Retrieval Optimization

Q01

What is reranking?

A second-stage retrieval step that reorders candidate chunks using a stronger relevance signal.

Q02

When does reranking help?

When the right evidence is retrieved but ranked too low for final context.

Q03

What can reranking not fix?

It cannot recover evidence missing from the candidate pool.

Q04

What is retrieve many, return few?

Retrieve a broad candidate pool, rerank it, then send a smaller high-quality context set.

Q05

Bi-encoder vs cross-encoder?

Bi-encoders are fast for search; cross-encoders read query and candidate together for slower but often better relevance scoring.

Q06

What metrics matter?

Candidate Recall@k, MRR, gold final rank, citation support, latency, timeout rate, and cost.

Q07

How do you manage latency?

Limit candidate pool, route reranking to high-value cases, use timeouts, and fallback to first-stage results.

Q08

Why dedupe after reranking?

To prevent repeated chunks from crowding out diverse evidence.

Q09

How do you release reranking?

Shadow test, compare before/after metrics, monitor latency, then roll out gradually.

Q10

What is the senior answer?

Reranking is useful when measured candidate recall is good but final ordering is weak.
