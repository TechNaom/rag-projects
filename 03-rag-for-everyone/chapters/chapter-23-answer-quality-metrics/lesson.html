Answer Quality Metrics

Retrieval metrics tell us whether evidence arrived. Answer quality metrics tell us whether the final response used that evidence correctly, helpfully, and safely.

What this chapter makes practical

  • Faithfulness: the answer must not say more than the evidence supports.
  • Groundedness: claims should be traceable to retrieved sources.
  • Answer relevance: the answer should actually address the user question.
  • Citation correctness: citations must support the sentence they decorate.
  • Human review: judgment is still needed for high-risk slices.

Sub-topic 1 of 10

Why Answer Quality Metrics Matter

A RAG system can retrieve the right chunk and still produce a bad answer. It may overstate the evidence, cite the wrong source, ignore the question, miss a caveat, or refuse when it should answer. Answer quality metrics sit after generation and inspect the response as a product artifact.

North starGood RAG answers are supported, useful, calibrated, cited, and safe.

Do not optimize for eloquence alone. A polished unsupported answer is worse than a plain grounded one.

01
Check factual support

Every factual claim should map to evidence.

02
Check usefulness

The answer should solve the user's actual question.

03
Check calibration

When evidence is weak, the answer should say so.

04
Check risk

High-risk domains need stricter human review and release gates.

Sub-topic 2 of 10

Faithfulness

Faithfulness asks: does the answer stay within the evidence? If evidence says "VP approval is required after 30 days" and the answer says "manager approval is enough," faithfulness failed.

High faithfulness

Answer repeats only supported facts and preserves conditions.

Low faithfulness

Answer adds unsupported assumptions, dates, policies, or guarantees.

Production check

Use claim extraction plus evidence support review for high-risk flows.

Sub-topic 3 of 10

Groundedness

Groundedness checks whether claims are traceable to retrieved context. It overlaps with faithfulness, but it focuses on source linkage and evidence coverage.

Sub-topic 4 of 10

Answer Relevance

An answer can be faithful but still not useful. If the user asks for refund exceptions and the answer explains the whole refund policy without naming exceptions, relevance is weak. Measure whether the answer resolves the user's task.

Sub-topic 5 of 10

Citation Correctness

Citation correctness means the cited source supports the sentence. Presence is not enough. This is where many production demos quietly fail.

Citation support ladder

citation present
  -> citation near claim
  -> cited source contains fact
  -> cited source is current and authoritative
  -> cited source supports the exact condition
Sub-topic 6 of 10

Human Review

Use human review for ambiguous, high-risk, or customer-impacting slices. Human review should use rubrics, not open-ended opinions.

Sub-topic 7 of 10

Rubrics

A strong rubric scores support, relevance, citation quality, completeness, tone, refusal correctness, and safety. Keep scores simple enough to apply consistently.

Sub-topic 8 of 10

Production Gates

Gate releases on high-risk faithfulness failures, citation-support regressions, false refusals, and known incident replays. Track metrics by prompt version, model version, retriever version, and domain.

Sub-topic 9 of 10

Common Mistakes

M01

Scoring only fluency

Pretty answers can be unsupported. Score support first.

M02

Trusting citations blindly

Check whether the cited text supports the exact claim.

M03

No false-refusal metric

Overly cautious assistants can damage adoption.

M04

No human-review rubric

Review becomes taste instead of repeatable evaluation.

Sub-topic 10 of 10

Project Roadmap

Build an answer quality scorer that receives question, evidence, answer, and citations. It should score faithfulness, relevance, citation support, refusal correctness, and release status.
