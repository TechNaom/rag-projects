Chunking Deep Dive

Chunking looks simple until retrieval quality collapses. This module shows why chunk boundaries are one of the highest-leverage decisions in RAG.
OutcomeChoose chunking strategies based on accuracy, latency, memory, and the shape of the source documents.
On this page1. Fixed-size chunking 2. Recursive chunking 3. Sentence chunking 4. Paragraph chunking 5. Semantic chunking 6. Sliding window chunking 7. Parent-child chunking 8. Hierarchical chunking

1. Fixed-size chunking

Fixed-size chunking matters because If you are building chunk strategy workbench, fixed-size chunking changes what you store, retrieve, or trust before answer generation.

Why it mattersChunking Deep Dive is easier to learn when you tie each concept to one concrete product behavior instead of memorizing jargon.

Example-driven view

Scenario: A banking policy assistant receives a user question. Fixed-size chunking determines how the system narrows evidence and how confidently it should answer.

  • Decision: What changes in the pipeline because of fixed-size chunking?
  • Trade-off: How does it affect latency, quality, and maintenance?
  • Interview angle: When would you choose this over a simpler default?
Quick recap: Fixed-size chunking is not just a term to memorize; it is a design choice you should be able to explain with a system example.
