📘 RAG for Everyone | Day 28/30

🏁 Twenty-seven chapters of individual pieces — chunking, embeddings, retrieval, prompting, metrics, safety, deployment. Chapter 28 is where they stop being individual pieces.

Today you'll learn:
✅ What the capstone chapter actually asks you to build
✅ Why "3 product builds + 5 specialized tracks" beats one generic project
✅ How this maps directly onto what Northkeep already proved

Not a review chapter — a build chapter 🔨
The capstone isn't a summary quiz. It's three production-grade product builds, plus five specialized capstone tracks to choose from — meant to force you to make every trade-off decision from chapters 2 through 27 yourself, on a real system, instead of just recognizing the right answer on a quiz.

Why the structure matters 🧩
Three product builds means you're not building one narrow example and calling it done — you're seeing how the same underlying discipline (grounded prompting, metadata design, retrieval tuning, safety, observability) applies differently depending on the product shape. Five specialized tracks means there's room to go deep on the part of the stack you actually care about most, instead of a one-size-fits-all final project.

The proof this isn't just theory 📊
This is exactly the same discipline that produced Northkeep National Bank's policy assistant: 12 source documents scoped deliberately, 111 chunks from a chosen chunking strategy, TF-IDF/SVD embeddings chosen for a real constraint, 86.7% retrieval accuracy measured against gold labels, 13 of 15 questions answered correctly, and every single answer traceable to its source document. That's not a hypothetical capstone outcome — it's what building through this exact sequence of decisions actually produces when you take each chapter seriously.

The bottom line 💡
A 28-chapter course is only as good as what it makes you capable of building unsupervised. The capstone is the test of that — not "did you understand RAG," but "can you make every one of these trade-offs yourself, on a system nobody hands you the answer key for."
