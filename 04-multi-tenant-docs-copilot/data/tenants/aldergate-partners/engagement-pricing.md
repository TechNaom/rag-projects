# Aldergate Partners — Engagement Pricing Guide

**Doc ID:** AP-PRC-002
**Audience:** Partners and engagement managers
**Owner:** Commercial Team
**Last updated:** January 2026

## 1. Pricing Models We Offer
Aldergate uses three pricing models: time-and-materials, fixed-fee, and a
capped time-and-materials hybrid. Time-and-materials suits discovery work with
unclear scope; fixed-fee suits well-defined deliverables; the capped hybrid
gives the client budget certainty while preserving flexibility on how hours are
spent. Retainers are available for ongoing advisory relationships.

## 2. Standard Day Rates
Standard blended day rates by role: Partner $3,200, Principal $2,400, Senior
Consultant $1,800, Consultant $1,200, and Analyst $800. Rates assume a standard
eight-hour consulting day. Rates are reviewed annually each January and are held
for the life of a signed statement of work.

## 3. Discount Authority
Engagement managers may offer up to a 10% discount on day rates without further
approval. Discounts between 10% and 20% require Principal sign-off. Any discount
above 20%, or any pro-bono work, requires Partner approval and must be justified
in writing against the strategic value of the relationship.

## 4. Expenses and Travel
Reasonable travel and expenses are billed at cost with no markup and must be
pre-approved by the client sponsor for any single item above $500. Consultants
book travel through Aldergate's travel desk to capture negotiated rates.
Expense receipts are submitted weekly; anything submitted more than 30 days
after it was incurred requires Partner approval to reimburse.

## 5. Invoicing and Payment Terms
Invoices are issued monthly in arrears against delivered milestones, with net-30
payment terms unless the statement of work specifies otherwise. Fixed-fee
engagements bill on a milestone schedule agreed at kickoff. Late payments beyond
60 days trigger the escalation process and may pause active delivery.

## 6. Change Orders
Any scope change that affects cost or timeline is handled through a written
change order signed by the client sponsor before the additional work begins.
Consultants must never absorb out-of-scope work informally; unbilled scope creep
is the most common cause of engagement margin erosion and is tracked as a
delivery risk.
