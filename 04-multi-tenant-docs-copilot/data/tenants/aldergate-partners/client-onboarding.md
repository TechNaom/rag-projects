# Aldergate Partners — Client Onboarding Playbook

**Doc ID:** AP-ONB-001
**Audience:** Engagement managers and consultants
**Owner:** Delivery Operations
**Last updated:** January 2026

## 1. Purpose
This playbook defines how Aldergate Partners brings a new client from a signed
statement of work to an active engagement within ten business days. Consistent
onboarding is the single biggest driver of client satisfaction scores in the
first quarter of any engagement, so these steps are mandatory, not optional.

## 2. Kickoff Within Five Business Days
The engagement manager must schedule a kickoff meeting within five business days
of contract signature. Attendees include the client sponsor, the client's day-
to-day contact, and the full Aldergate delivery team. The agenda covers scope
confirmation, success metrics, the communication cadence, and the RACI matrix
for decision rights.

## 3. Access and Tooling
By the end of week one, the client must provision Aldergate consultants with the
access they need: shared document space, relevant read-only systems, and a
communication channel. Aldergate never stores client data on personal devices;
all work products live in the client-designated workspace or Aldergate's
approved, access-controlled project drive.

## 4. Defining Success Metrics
Every engagement is anchored to three to five measurable success metrics agreed
with the client sponsor during kickoff. Metrics must be specific and time-bound
(for example, "reduce invoice processing time from 9 days to 4 days by end of
quarter"). Vague goals such as "improve efficiency" are not accepted as success
metrics.

## 5. The First Status Report
The engagement manager sends the first weekly status report at the end of week
one, even if the work is still ramping up. The report follows the standard
template: progress against milestones, decisions needed from the client, risks
and mitigations, and planned activities for the coming week. Establishing this
rhythm early sets the tone for the whole engagement.

## 6. Onboarding Sign-Off
Onboarding is considered complete when the client sponsor confirms in writing
that scope, metrics, and cadence are agreed, and the delivery team has working
access to everything required. The engagement manager records the sign-off date
in the engagement tracker; this date starts the clock for the first billing
milestone.
