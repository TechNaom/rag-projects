# Aldergate Partners — Deliverable Standards

**Doc ID:** AP-DEL-003
**Audience:** All consultants
**Owner:** Delivery Operations
**Last updated:** February 2026

## 1. What Counts as a Deliverable
A deliverable is any work product formally handed to the client: a report, a
model, a workshop, a prototype, or a recommendation memo. Internal working
documents are not deliverables. Every deliverable listed in the statement of
work has a named owner and a due date in the engagement tracker.

## 2. The Two-Reviewer Rule
No deliverable leaves Aldergate without two reviews: a peer review by another
consultant for accuracy and logic, and a quality review by the engagement
manager or a Principal for narrative, client fit, and firm standards. Both
reviews are recorded in the tracker. Skipping review to hit a deadline is never
acceptable; if a deadline is at risk, escalate rather than ship unreviewed work.

## 3. Structure and Narrative
Client-facing documents lead with the answer, not the analysis. Every report
opens with an executive summary that states the recommendation in the first
paragraph, followed by the supporting evidence. Aldergate follows a "so what"
discipline: every chart and table must carry a one-line takeaway explaining why
it matters to the client's decision.

## 4. Data and Source Integrity
Every quantitative claim in a deliverable must be traceable to a documented
source. Consultants maintain a source log alongside each model so any number can
be defended if the client challenges it. Estimates and assumptions are labeled
clearly as such and never presented as measured fact.

## 5. Branding and Formatting
Deliverables use the current Aldergate template with the client's name and the
engagement code on the cover. Fonts, color palette, and chart styling follow the
firm style guide. Draft documents are watermarked "DRAFT" until the engagement
manager approves the final version for release.

## 6. Handover and Archiving
On final delivery, the engagement manager walks the client through the
deliverable rather than emailing it cold. A copy of every released deliverable
is archived in the engagement's project drive within two business days, with
access restricted to the delivery team and the responsible Partner for
confidentiality.
