# Riverbend Trust — Grant Reporting Requirements

**Doc ID:** RT-RPT-003
**Program:** Community Resilience Grants
**Audience:** Active grantees
**Last updated:** February 2026

## 1. Reporting Philosophy
Riverbend asks for reporting that is useful to the grantee, not just to the
Trust. Reports should help an organization reflect on what is working and adjust,
which is why the Trust favors honest narrative over polished spin. A report that
describes a setback and what was learned is more valuable than one that claims
everything went perfectly.

## 2. Interim Report
Grantees submit one interim report at the midpoint of the grant period. The
interim report is brief: progress against the outcomes named in the proposal,
any changes to the plan, and how funds have been spent so far. It is due within
two weeks of the midpoint date stated in the grant agreement.

## 3. Final Report
A final report is due 60 days after the end of the grant period. It covers
outcomes achieved against the logic model, a full financial accounting of how
the grant was spent, stories or data illustrating impact, and lessons learned.
Submitting the final report on time is a prerequisite for any future funding
from the Trust.

## 4. Financial Accounting
The financial section reconciles actual spending against the approved budget.
Variances of more than 10% in any budget line must be explained. Unspent funds
at the end of the grant period must either be returned or, with written approval
from the grants office, carried into a no-cost extension of up to three months.

## 5. Outcome Measurement
Grantees report against the specific, measurable outcomes committed in the
proposal. The Trust does not expect every target to be met; it expects an honest
account of what was achieved and why. Where an outcome fell short, the report
should explain the cause and what the organization would do differently.

## 6. Site Visits and Recognition
The Trust may request one site visit during the grant period, scheduled at least
two weeks in advance at the grantee's convenience. Grantees are asked to
acknowledge Riverbend Trust support in public materials about the funded work,
but the Trust does not require its logo on every document and never requires
exclusive attribution.
