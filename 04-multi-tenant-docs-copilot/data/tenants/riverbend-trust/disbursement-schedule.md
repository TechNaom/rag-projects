# Riverbend Trust — Grant Disbursement Schedule

**Doc ID:** RT-DIS-004
**Program:** Community Resilience Grants
**Audience:** Active grantees and finance staff
**Last updated:** February 2026

## 1. Disbursement Overview
Grant funds are released against a schedule set in the signed grant agreement.
Riverbend disburses by electronic transfer to the organization's operating
account within ten business days of each milestone being met. Funds are never
released before the grant agreement is countersigned by both parties.

## 2. Standard Two-Payment Structure
Most grants use a two-payment structure: 60% of the award is released on
execution of the grant agreement, and the remaining 40% is released after the
interim report is received and accepted. This front-loads working capital so
organizations are not forced to spend their own reserves before reimbursement.

## 3. Multi-Year Grants
Multi-year awards are disbursed one year at a time. Each year's payment is
contingent on the prior year's final report being submitted and accepted. The
Trust confirms the coming year's funding in writing at least one month before
the next disbursement so grantees can plan their cash flow.

## 4. Holdbacks and Conditions
The Trust may hold back up to 20% of an award pending a specific condition, such
as securing matching funds or hiring a key role. Holdback conditions are stated
explicitly in the grant agreement, and the held funds are released promptly once
the grants office confirms the condition has been met.

## 5. Late Reports and Paused Payments
If a required report is overdue, scheduled payments are paused until the report
is received. The grants office notifies the grantee before pausing a payment and
works with organizations facing genuine hardship. Persistent failure to report,
without communication, may result in termination of the grant and recovery of
unspent funds.

## 6. No-Cost Extensions
A grantee that needs more time may request a no-cost extension of up to three
months in writing before the grant period ends. Extensions change the reporting
and any remaining disbursement dates accordingly but never increase the total
award. Only one no-cost extension is granted per award except in extraordinary
circumstances.
