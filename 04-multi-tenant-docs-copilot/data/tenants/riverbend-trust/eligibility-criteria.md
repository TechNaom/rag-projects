# Riverbend Trust — Grant Eligibility Criteria

**Doc ID:** RT-ELG-001
**Program:** Community Resilience Grants
**Audience:** Prospective applicants
**Last updated:** January 2026

## 1. Who May Apply
The Riverbend Trust funds registered nonprofit organizations and fiscally
sponsored projects operating within the Riverbend watershed region. Applicants
must have been legally established for at least two years and be able to
demonstrate active programming during that time. Individuals, for-profit
businesses, and political campaigns are not eligible.

## 2. Geographic Scope
Funded work must primarily benefit residents of the five counties in the
Riverbend watershed. Organizations headquartered outside the region may apply
only if the funded project is delivered inside the region and led by local
staff or partners. National organizations must apply through their local
chapter, not their central office.

## 3. Funding Priorities
Each grant cycle prioritizes three areas: environmental stewardship of the
river and its tributaries, food security for low-income households, and youth
education programs. Projects that advance more than one priority are welcome but
are not scored higher solely for breadth; depth of impact matters more than the
number of priorities touched.

## 4. Grant Sizes
The Trust awards grants from $10,000 to $75,000 per year. First-time applicants
are typically funded at $25,000 or below to establish a track record. Multi-year
support of up to three years is available by invitation to organizations that
have successfully completed at least one prior Riverbend grant and met their
reporting obligations.

## 5. Ineligible Costs
Grant funds may not be used for endowments, deficit reduction, lobbying,
religious proselytizing, or capital construction exceeding 20% of the grant.
Reasonable administrative overhead is allowed up to 15% of the total award. Any
budget that allocates more than 15% to overhead will be returned for revision
before review.

## 6. Conflict of Interest
Organizations with a board member or senior staff member who is related to a
Riverbend trustee must disclose the relationship in the application. Disclosure
does not disqualify an applicant, but an undisclosed conflict discovered later
is grounds for withdrawing an award and recovering disbursed funds.
