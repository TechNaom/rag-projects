# Riverbend Trust — Grant Application Process

**Doc ID:** RT-APP-002
**Program:** Community Resilience Grants
**Audience:** Prospective applicants
**Last updated:** January 2026

## 1. Two-Stage Application
Riverbend uses a two-stage process. Stage one is a short letter of inquiry;
stage two is a full proposal by invitation only. This protects applicants from
writing a full proposal before the Trust confirms the project is a fit. Roughly
one in three letters of inquiry are invited to submit a full proposal.

## 2. Letter of Inquiry
The letter of inquiry is a maximum of two pages and describes the organization,
the proposed project, the population served, and the amount requested. Letters
are accepted on a rolling basis and reviewed within four weeks. There is no fee
to apply, and letters may be submitted through the online portal or by email to
the grants office.

## 3. Full Proposal
Invited applicants have six weeks to submit a full proposal, including a project
narrative, a line-item budget, a logic model linking activities to outcomes, and
the organization's most recent financial statement. Proposals are reviewed by a
committee of trustees and community advisors that meets quarterly.

## 4. Review Criteria
Proposals are scored on four criteria weighted equally: alignment with funding
priorities, clarity and realism of the plan, organizational capacity to deliver,
and a credible plan to measure outcomes. Reviewers look for honest treatment of
risks; a proposal that ignores obvious challenges scores lower than one that
names them and plans for them.

## 5. Decision and Notification
Funding decisions are made at the quarterly committee meeting and communicated
within two weeks of the meeting. Declined applicants receive brief written
feedback and are welcome to reapply in a future cycle. Approved applicants
receive a grant agreement that must be signed before any funds are released.

## 6. Deadlines
Letters of inquiry are accepted year-round. Full proposals must arrive at least
three weeks before a quarterly committee meeting to be considered in that cycle;
the committee meets in March, June, September, and December. Proposals arriving
inside the three-week window roll to the next quarter's meeting.
